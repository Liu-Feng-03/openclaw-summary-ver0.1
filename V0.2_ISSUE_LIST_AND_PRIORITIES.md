## 1. Purpose

This document turns the current product review into an execution-oriented issue list.

Its purpose is to help future design and development processes quickly answer:
- what is wrong in v0.1
- why it matters
- what should be fixed first
- what can wait until later

This is not a bug list in the engineering sense.

It is a **product / UX / IA / visual / interaction issue inventory**.

---

## 2. Priority Scale

### P0 — Must fix in first v0.2 rebuild
If not fixed, the core product experience will still feel incomplete or confusing.

### P1 — Should fix in v0.2 core cycle
Important improvements that strongly affect product quality, but do not block the rebuild skeleton.

### P2 — Good improvement after core rebuild
Useful and valuable, but can wait until the main experience architecture is stable.

### P3 — Later polish / enhancement
Nice to have, branding polish, or dependent on future complexity.

---

## 3. Severity Scale

### Critical
Strongly harms comprehension, navigation, or overall product quality.

### High
Creates noticeable friction, repetition, or confusion.

### Medium
Does not break the product, but weakens polish or efficiency.

### Low
Minor enhancement or quality upgrade.

## A. Product Positioning in UI

### A1. The interface still feels more like a docs site than a learning product
- Severity: Critical
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - The product direction is correct, but the UI expression still resembles a structured static documentation site.
  - Users can read, but they are not strongly guided.
- Why it matters:
  - This is the root issue behind weak onboarding, weak flow, and weak product differentiation.
- Suggested fix:
  - redesign around guided entry, progressive disclosure, and stronger page-type differentiation
  - treat the site as a learning product, not a page collection

### A2. The learning path exists in content strategy but not strongly enough in UI
- Severity: Critical
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - The roadmap emphasizes learning order, but the interface does not embody it clearly enough.
- Why it matters:
  - Users must infer the intended route instead of being gently led through it.
- Suggested fix:
  - make pathing a first-class UI element
  - show recommended sequence, next step, and role in learning journey

---

## B. Homepage

### B1. Homepage behaves like a directory, not a routing page
- Severity: Critical
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - The homepage exposes many options, but does not strongly prioritize the best first action.
- Why it matters:
  - New users land in a choice-heavy state too early.
- Suggested fix:
  - redesign homepage around routing: by stage, by goal, by module
  - add a dominant primary CTA

### B2. Homepage information density is broad but not sharply sequenced
- Severity: High
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - multiple sections are individually valid, but together feel flat and parallel rather than guided
- Why it matters:
  - users may scan without understanding how to begin
- Suggested fix:
  - restructure homepage order:
    1. product definition
    2. primary route choice
    3. guided learning path
    4. free exploration
    5. shareable deep links

### B3. Homepage currently asks users to make too many equal-weight decisions
- Severity: High
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - entry cards and sections compete too much for attention
- Why it matters:
  - weakens confidence and increases cognitive load
- Suggested fix:
  - reduce visible decision count above the fold
  - bundle options into clearer route groups

## C. Page-Type Differentiation

### C1. Different module pages look too similar to each other
- Severity: Critical
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - Getting Started, Concepts, Setup, Use Cases, FAQ, and Advanced mostly share the same page structure and visual rhythm
- Why it matters:
  - weak module identity
  - weak memory of what each area does
  - “template clone” feeling
- Suggested fix:
  - define distinct page archetypes for each module type
  - use different hero behaviors, layout emphasis, and interaction styles

### C2. Module pages do not feel like distinct learning environments
- Severity: High
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - modules differ in content, but not enough in experience logic
- Why it matters:
  - users do not strongly feel they are entering a new mode (onboarding / concept map / setup flow / FAQ triage)
- Suggested fix:
  - create module-specific interaction patterns and visual language

### C3. Page purpose is not always immediately obvious from layout alone
- Severity: High
- Priority: P1
- Problem:
  - users may not instantly know whether a page is for overview, pathing, diagnosis, or deep reading
- Why it matters:
  - increases interpretation burden
- Suggested fix:
  - make page purpose explicit in page header and section logic

## D. Hero / Above-the-Fold Design

### D1. Hero design is too heavy for most subpages
- Severity: High
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - subpages use a large, landing-page-like hero pattern that feels oversized for module hubs
- Why it matters:
  - makes each module feel visually overblown
  - adds unnecessary repetition and top-of-page weight
- Suggested fix:
  - keep stronger hero only where needed
  - compress module-page headers into more functional, lower-friction summaries

### D2. Main heading text in the left hero panel is visually too large / dominant
- Severity: High
- Priority: P1
- Problem:
  - typography weight is too strong relative to page role, especially on non-home pages
- Why it matters:
  - reduces scanning comfort and makes pages feel overly similar
- Suggested fix:
  - reduce heading size on module pages
  - vary header hierarchy by page type

### D3. Right-side hero KPI block is repetitive and low-value
- Severity: Critical
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - the same KPI block repeats across pages with little page-specific value
- Why it matters:
  - wastes prime screen space
  - increases template-clone feeling
  - weakens trust in information density
- Suggested fix:
  - replace with module-specific helper panel, or remove entirely

### D4. Left/right hero balance is weak
- Severity: Medium
- Priority: P1
- Problem:
  - left panel is heavy, right panel is visually lighter but occupies meaningful space without enough value
- Why it matters:
  - creates imbalance and weakens first-screen efficiency
- Suggested fix:
  - simplify or repurpose the right panel into contextual support content

## E. Navigation and Wayfinding

### E1. Lack of breadcrumb on subpages
- Severity: Critical
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - users can enter deep pages without clear location awareness
- Why it matters:
  - especially harmful in a multi-page learning site
- Suggested fix:
  - add breadcrumb to all module and detail pages

### E2. Lack of “where am I / what is this / what next” structure
- Severity: Critical
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - many pages do not explicitly answer the core orientation questions
- Why it matters:
  - users can read content but still feel directionless
- Suggested fix:
  - enforce page-level orientation blocks and next-step guidance

### E3. Top navigation is website navigation, not learning navigation
- Severity: High
- Priority: P1
- Problem:
  - current nav helps jumping between modules, but not moving through learning flow
- Why it matters:
  - the product does not yet feel like a guided system
- Suggested fix:
  - keep top nav, but add learning-path signals and local navigation helpers

### E4. Lack of previous / next navigation between related pages
- Severity: High
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - users finish one page and are not smoothly carried forward
- Why it matters:
  - increases abandonment and backtracking
- Suggested fix:
  - add previous / next page navigation to detail pages and key module pages

### E5. Lack of consistent “recommended next step” block
- Severity: High
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - users are not always told what to read next
- Why it matters:
  - breaks the intended learning path
- Suggested fix:
  - add a standard next-step module to detail pages

## F. Card System

### F1. Cards currently function mostly as jump links
- Severity: Critical
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - cards rarely provide enough preview value before forcing navigation
- Why it matters:
  - creates click anxiety and high browsing friction
- Suggested fix:
  - redesign cards as preview / expandable units

### F2. Cards lack metadata that would make them easier to choose
- Severity: High
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - no visible audience / difficulty / reading time / prerequisites / tags
- Why it matters:
  - users cannot quickly judge suitability
- Suggested fix:
  - add first-class metadata display to cards and pages

### F3. Card internals are too thin and repetitive
- Severity: High
- Priority: P1
- Problem:
  - title + one sentence + “查看页面” is too uniform across the site
- Why it matters:
  - weakens information density and interaction richness
- Suggested fix:
  - support summary, tags, prerequisites, relation hints, and alternate actions

### F4. Card role differences are not clearly expressed
- Severity: Medium
- Priority: P1
- Problem:
  - route card, topic card, scenario card, and FAQ card feel too similar
- Why it matters:
  - component semantics are blurred
- Suggested fix:
  - define card subtypes with different structure and behavior

## G. Interaction Model

### G1. The site relies too heavily on hard page transitions
- Severity: Critical
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - most user actions require leaving the current page
- Why it matters:
  - harms browsing continuity and perceived usability
- Suggested fix:
  - introduce expansion, preview, and partial in-page exploration first

### G2. There is no meaningful middle state between “see a title” and “open a full page”
- Severity: Critical
- Priority: P0
- Problem:
  - users cannot inspect content before committing to a navigation jump
- Why it matters:
  - weakens safety, confidence, and exploration flow
- Suggested fix:
  - add accordion / expandable cards / summary drawers

### G3. The product lacks continuous browsing behavior
- Severity: High
- Priority: P1
- Problem:
  - the site feels segmented into pages rather than connected into a learning surface
- Why it matters:
  - reduces product polish and lowers session depth
- Suggested fix:
  - connect module pages and detail pages more intelligently

## 8. Short Handoff Summary

If another design or development process reads only one section of this file, the key takeaway is:

> v0.1 does not mainly suffer from missing content.
> It mainly suffers from insufficient differentiation, insufficient orientation, too much jump friction, and too much repeated template structure.

Therefore the first v0.2 rebuild should focus on:
- page-type differentiation
- guided routing
- expandable previews
- stronger navigation and next-step logic
- module-specific experience patterns
- lower repetition in above-the-fold design
